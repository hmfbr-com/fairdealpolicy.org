FDP Template Skeleton v1.0

Canonical commitment grammar for FDP-compatible templates.

This is structure-first. It does not prescribe domain-specific policy content. It prescribes how fair-deal commitments must be structured to stay coherent while evolving.

0. Identification & versioning

  • Template name
  • Template family (if applicable)
  • Skeleton version referenced
  • Template version
  • Date of issuance
  • Intended scope of application
  • Explicit references to relevant FDP Articles

1. Principles & constraints

  • FDP Articles explicitly referenced
  • Non-negotiable principles
  • Declared fairness assumptions
  • Explicit exclusions (what this template does not govern)

2. Actors, scope & power surfaces

  • Defined actors and roles
  • Rights and responsibilities per actor
  • Declared power asymmetries
  • Resource, data, or decision control points
  • Entry and exit conditions

3. Outset design (pre-commit lens)

This block is required. If absent or superficial, FDP alignment is invalid.

  • Mandatory pre-commit questions
  • Disclosure requirements
  • Decision thresholds
  • Red-flag or no-go conditions
  • References to applicable FDP outset formulas (if used)

4. Governance, dispute & correction

  • Governance mechanism
  • Dispute resolution process
  • Escalation paths
  • Correction and remediation logic
  • Abuse and misuse handling

5. Review, learning & evolution

  • Review cadence
  • Feedback incorporation method
  • Update rules for the template
  • Relationship to Skeleton updates
  • Conditions for deprecation or sunset

Validity

A template is FDP-compatible if and only if all blocks are present, each block fulfills its purpose, FDP Article references are explicit, outset design is substantively applied, and versioning is unambiguous.

Evolution rules

  • Skeleton versioning: MAJOR = structural changes (rare), MINOR = clarifications.
  • Template versioning: independent, but must reference the Skeleton version used.
